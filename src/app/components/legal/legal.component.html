Site specific notices

The individual who creates a website is not obliged to reveal his identity. However, he must communicate to the host of his site the elements which will allow him to be identified if necessary. These identification elements are covered by professional secrecy. But the website host has the obligation to disclose them in the context of legal proceedings.

If the individual chooses to remain anonymous, the only mandatory information to be found on his website are: name, name or company name and address and telephone number of the host of his website.

If the individual does not wish to remain anonymous, he must indicate the legal notices concerning himself and those of the host of his website.

A cookie is a small computer file which makes it possible to analyze the behavior of users when visiting a website or using software or a mobile application.

Publishers of sites or applications that use cookies must:

  • Inform internet users of the purpose of cookies,
  • Obtain their consent,
  • Provide Internet users with a way to refuse them.

The period of validity of the consent given in this context is 13 months maximum.
